TCG Scam Awareness

The Pokémon TCG market can be incredibly lucrative, which unfortunately attracts scammers. Learn how to protect yourself and your investments with our guide to common scams.

WARNING: If a deal seems too good to be true, it almost certainly is. Always buy from reputable sellers or authorized local game stores (LGS).

Resealed Packs

Scammers will open booster packs, remove the valuable "hit" cards, replace them with bulk, and then carefully reseal the packaging using heat guns or glue.

How to spot:

  • Excessive glue or residue on the flaps.
  • Uneven crimping on the top or bottom of the pack.
  • Small tears or holes in the corners.

Pack Weighing

In older sets, holographic cards weighed slightly more than regular cards. Scammers would use a digital scale to find the "heavy" packs and sell the "light" ones to unsuspecting buyers.

Note: Modern sets use code cards of different thicknesses to offset the weight of holographic cards, making weighing much more difficult, but not always impossible depending on the set.

Counterfeit Cards

Fake cards are produced in mass quantities. See our Card Detection page for a visual guide.

Fake Graded Slabs

Even graded cards (PSA, Beckett, CGC) are faked. Scammers crack open real slabs, insert a fake card, and seal them, or they create entirely fake plastic slabs with fake labels.

How to spot:

  • Check the certification number on the grading company's official website.
  • Look for frosting or cracks on the edges of the plastic case.
  • Examine the font and holographic elements on the label itself.
